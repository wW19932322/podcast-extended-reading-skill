# Novice Explanation Patterns

Read this file whenever the user says they are a beginner in the outside field.

The goal is not to dilute the content. The goal is to change the explanation order so a beginner can build the right mental model before the jargon arrives.

## Research Anchors

Use these learning-science anchors to guide the writing style:

1. Worked examples help novices more than immediate problem solving.
   Source: Sweller, J., & Cooper, G. A. 1985. *The use of worked examples as a substitute for problem solving in learning algebra*. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1207/s1532690xci0201_3)
   Why it matters here: Start by showing one solved or fully interpreted case before asking the reader to juggle abstractions.

2. Explanations that help novices can become redundant or harmful for experts.
   Source: Kalyuga, S., Ayres, P., Chandler, P., & Sweller, J. 2003. *The expertise reversal effect*. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1207/S15326985EP3801_4)
   Why it matters here: Beginner mode should add scaffolding on purpose, not by accident; advanced mode should compress it.

3. Comparing examples helps learners abstract the deeper relational structure.
   Source: Gentner, D., Loewenstein, J., & Thompson, L. 2003. *Learning and transfer: A general role for analogical encoding*. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-0663.95.2.393)
   Why it matters here: When one analogy is not enough, compare two cases and name the shared structure explicitly.

4. Concrete supports are useful, but learners often need a gradual move from concrete to abstract.
   Source: Fyfe, E. R., McNeil, N. M., Son, J. Y., & Goldstone, R. L. 2014. *Concreteness fading in mathematics and science instruction: A systematic review*. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10648-014-9249-3)
   Why it matters here: Use vivid analogies first, then strip away surface details and restate the formal concept.

5. Concreteness fading is helpful but not universally automatic; the transition has to be designed carefully.
   Source: Fyfe, E. R., de Leeuw, J. R., Carvalho, P. F., Goldstone, R. L., & Motz, B. A. 2021. *Exemplary pathways to relational learning: A review of the influence of concreteness on symbolic understanding*. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10648-020-09581-7)
   Why it matters here: Do not let the analogy swallow the concept. Always come back to the abstract relation.

## Default Beginner Sequence

When explaining a hard concept to a beginner, prefer this sequence:

1. `Concrete entry`
   Use a vivid case, anecdote, or simple scenario from the podcast or from the outside field.

2. `Worked interpretation`
   Show how to read that case step by step. Do not ask the user to infer the mechanism alone.

3. `Second comparison`
   Add a second case or analogy and state what the two cases share.

4. `Abstract lift`
   Name the mechanism, model, or formal concept only after the shared structure is visible.

5. `Analogy boundary`
   Say clearly where the analogy stops working.

6. `Formal restatement`
   Restate the same idea in the field's professional language.

7. `Self-check`
   End with one short question that lets the user test whether they understood the mechanism.

## Analogy Rules

- Use analogies to preserve structure, not just vibe.
- After every important analogy, write:
  - `这个类比里，A 对应的是...`
  - `它不适用的地方是...`
- Prefer process analogies over object analogies when the concept is dynamic.
- If the concept is especially abstract, use two analogies rather than one when possible.

## Style Rules For Beginner Mode

- Define terms only when they become necessary.
- Prefer one deep paragraph over five shallow labels.
- Avoid stacking three new abstractions in one sentence.
- When using jargon, immediately translate it into an observable action, relation, or failure mode.
- Explain what problem the concept was invented to solve.

## Mini Templates

### Template A: Hard paper for a beginner

- `先看一个具体场景`
- `这篇研究真正想解决的问题是`
- `它怎么做这个实验/识别`
- `所以结果说明的不是“X 总是真的”，而是“在什么条件下，X 更可能成立”`
- `如果换成更专业的话，它其实在讨论`

### Template B: Cross-domain bridge for a beginner

- `播客里的这句原文在说什么`
- `在目标领域里，最接近它的问题是什么`
- `先用一个类比说明`
- `再指出类比的边界`
- `最后给出正式概念和来源`

## Failure Modes

Avoid these common mistakes:

- using analogy without mapping
- leaving the reader inside the example and never returning to the concept
- replacing explanation with simplification slogans
- giving expert compression to a novice
- adding decorative philosophy before the mechanism is clear
