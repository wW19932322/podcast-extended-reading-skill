## Writing Rules

- Lead with the episode anchor before the outside material.
- Keep the bridging sentence explicit. Never assume the connection is obvious.
- Use short direct source links.
- Keep quotes short and use them as retrieval handles.
- Say when a conclusion is your synthesis rather than the source's own claim.
- For quantitative items, surface the deciding numbers before the interpretation paragraph.
- Only include visuals that help the reader understand faster than a compact table would.
- In beginner mode, use concrete entry before abstract compression.
- When using analogy, always name both the mapping and the boundary.
